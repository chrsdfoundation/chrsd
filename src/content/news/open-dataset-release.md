FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE — Dhaka, Bangladesh

The Centre for Humanitarian Research and Social Development (CHRSD) Foundation has
released an open, anonymised dataset drawn from 6,200 rural household resilience
surveys conducted across four divisions between 2024 and 2026.

The dataset is published under an open licence and is intended to support independent
researchers, government planners, and partner organisations working on rural poverty,
climate adaptation, and social protection. All personally identifying information has
been removed in line with CHRSD's data governance and consent protocols.

"Evidence held privately helps one organisation. Evidence shared openly helps a
sector," said the head of CHRSD's research division. The Foundation intends to release
updated datasets annually.

For media enquiries, contact info@chrsd.org.
