The CHRSD Foundation today announced the launch of a three-year Climate Resilience
Programme spanning the coastal districts of Satkhira, Khulna, and Bagerhat. The
initiative will reach an estimated 40,000 households with a combination of
early-warning literacy training, salinity-tolerant livelihood support, and
community-led embankment maintenance.

The programme builds directly on evidence gathered through CHRSD's socio-economic
research over the previous two years, which identified early-warning comprehension —
not warning availability — as the decisive factor in household-level outcomes during
tidal surges.

Activities will begin in the dry season, with the first cohort of community
early-warning facilitators completing training ahead of the next monsoon.
