An independent monitoring review has confirmed that 82% of the one million saplings
planted through CHRSD's Community Tree Planting programme in 2025 survived their first
twelve months — significantly above the regional reforestation benchmark of roughly
50%.

The result is attributed to three programme design choices: matching species to
micro-site soil moisture, assigning a single named household steward to each plot, and
scheduling intensive follow-up during the critical first dry season rather than at
planting.

CHRSD will publish the full monitoring methodology and plot-level data through its
Impact & Evidence portal later this quarter.
