The number that makes a good headline — *one million saplings planted* — is rarely
the number that matters. After three planting seasons across the Madhupur tract, the
metric our field teams now lead with is twelve-month survival, and it tells a very
different story.

## Survival is a design problem, not a luck problem

Where we matched species to soil moisture and handed stewardship to a named household
rather than a committee, survival climbed past 80%. Where saplings went in fast to hit
a planting target, less than half were alive a year later.

## What we changed

- **Species mix first.** We now plant a documented blend of natives suited to each micro-site.
- **One steward per plot.** Accountability beats good intentions.
- **Dry-season follow-up.** The first April is when most losses happen — so that is when we show up.

Reforestation is slow, unglamorous, and deeply local. The watershed does not care how
many saplings we put in the ground. It responds to how many are still standing when the
rains return.
