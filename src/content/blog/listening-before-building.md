A survey that arrives before trust does will collect answers, but not truth. Our
research team treats the first visit to any community as a conversation, not a
questionnaire.

## The first visit collects nothing

No clipboards, no tablets. We sit, we introduce the work, and we listen to what people
think the problem actually is. More than once, this has reshaped the study entirely —
the question we came to ask turned out to be the wrong one.

## Designing instruments people want to answer

When the survey does come, it is short, in plain Bangla, and explains exactly how each
answer will be used. We share findings back with the community before they reach any
donor report. Research that extracts data and disappears erodes the very trust the next
study will need.

Good evidence is a relationship, not a transaction.
