Resilience is rarely built during a flood. By then the choices have already been made —
or not made — months earlier, in the quiet season when the danger feels abstract.

## The work that doesn't make the news

Raising a plinth. Mapping the safest route to higher ground. Teaching a household to
read a colour-coded flag warning. None of it photographs like a rescue, but it is the
difference between a disruption and a disaster.

## Early-warning literacy

A warning system only works if the last household understands it. We focus on that last
household — the one furthest from the embankment, often headed by a woman, often the one
the formal system reaches last. When she can interpret the warning herself, she does not
wait for permission to move.

The water will come. The question we work on is what the community already knows by the
time it does.
